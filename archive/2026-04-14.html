Students Launch Independent Magazines After University Suspends Campus Publications

Alabama Reflector / The Crimson White April 13, 2026 Mixed

Former staff of the university-suspended magazines Nineteen Fifty-Six and Alice have launched two independent replacements, Selene and Sixty-Three, funded by nearly $30,000 raised through MASTHEAD, an alumni nonprofit. The new publications debuted at Druid City Brewing Company this past weekend. The move follows the university's December 2025 decision to permanently defund the student publications, citing a non-binding U.S. Attorney General memo on DEI-related programming.

Federal First Amendment Lawsuit Over Suspended Magazines Draws National Attention

Inside Higher Ed / NAACP LDF / ACLU March 25, 2026 Critical

Eight students have filed a federal lawsuit against the UA president and board of trustees, arguing that suspending the two campus magazines violates their First Amendment rights. Represented by the Southern Poverty Law Center, the NAACP Legal Defense Fund, and the ACLU of Alabama, the plaintiffs contend that the Bondi memo imposes no binding requirements and cannot override constitutional protections. Coverage in Inside Higher Ed and the Student Press Law Center has elevated the story to a national higher-education audience.

Engineers Use World's Only Quad-Barrel Debris Cannon to Build Tornado-Proof Shelters

UA News Center April 2026 Positive

UA engineering faculty are developing modular "SmartGuard" shelters designed to withstand two-by-four lumber launched at approximately 130 mph from the university's one-of-a-kind quad-barrel debris cannon. The research, rooted in lessons from the devastating 2011 Tuscaloosa tornado, aims to create low-cost, retail-ready storm protection and to inform updated building codes across tornado-prone regions.
